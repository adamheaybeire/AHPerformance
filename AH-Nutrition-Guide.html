3
Daily Nutrition Framework
A simple structure to help you build balanced meals throughout the day. This isn't a rigid meal plan — it's a framework to guide your choices.

Breakfast

Protein sourcee.g. eggs, Greek yoghurt, protein oats
Complex carbe.g. oats, wholegrain toast, fruit
Healthy fate.g. avocado, nuts, seeds
Target~25% of daily calories

Lunch

Protein sourcee.g. chicken, fish, tofu, lentils
Complex carbe.g. rice, sweet potato, pasta, bread
VegetablesAim for half the plate — any colour
Target~30% of daily calories

Dinner

Protein sourcee.g. meat, fish, eggs, beans
Complex carbe.g. potatoes, rice, quinoa
VegetablesAim for half the plate — variety is key
Target~30% of daily calories

Snacks (1-2 per day)

OptionsFruit, nuts, yoghurt, rice cakes, protein bar
Target~15% of daily calories
General principles: Prioritise whole foods over processed. Stay hydrated (aim for 2-3 litres of water daily). Eat a protein source with every meal. Don't skip meals — consistency matters more than perfection. If you're training hard, time your largest carb meal around your workout.

Recipe Guidance

Quick Reference: Portion Sizes

Protein (per meal)Palm-sized portion (~30-40g)
Carbs (per meal)Cupped-hand portion (~40-60g)
Fats (per meal)Thumb-sized portion (~10-15g)
Vegetables (per meal)Fist-sized portion or more
Hand-based portions are a practical, no-scales-needed approach. They're not perfectly accurate but they're consistent and easy to use anywhere — at home, at work, or eating out.
